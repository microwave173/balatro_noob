# Balatro White Stake Beginner Guide

> Goal: this guide is not about chasing endless-mode high scores. It is about **clearing the White Stake Ante 8 Boss Blind more consistently**.  
> Intended for: new players, players who often die around Ante 3–6, and anyone building a rule-based or LLM-based Balatro agent baseline.

---

## 0. Core Idea for Clearing White Stake

White Stake does not require fancy infinite combos. A stable clear usually comes from three things:

1. **Survive the early game**: In Antes 1–2, get at least one Joker that directly improves scoring.
2. **Build an economy and choose a direction in the mid game**: Do not spend all your money every shop. Focus on one or two main poker hands.
3. **By the late game, you usually need all three scoring components: `Chips + Mult + XMult`**. Stacking only one type of score source often stops working later.

Balatro's basic scoring logic is:

```text
Final score ≈ Chips × Mult
```

This means that only adding Chips or only adding Mult will eventually hit a ceiling. In the late game, `XMult` is especially important because it multiplies the Mult you have already built up.

---

## 1. Ante 1: Use Flushes and Stable Hands to Survive

### 1.1 Do not overcomplicate the first blind

At the very start, you usually have no Joker support. Your realistic goal is to beat the first blind with poker hands that have decent base value.

Common early options:

- **Flush**: Very stable early, easy to assemble, and has a decent base score.
- **Full House**: Good if your opening hand already has a Three of a Kind or Two Pair structure.
- **Straight**: Strong when it naturally appears, but do not chase it too hard if you have very few outs.

A common beginner-friendly approach is to default toward Flushes early rather than forcing Straights. If your hand already contains a Three of a Kind, a Full House can also be a strong choice.

### 1.2 When should you reset?

On White Stake, you do not need to reset too aggressively.  
Even if the Ante 1 shop is bad, you can usually continue into Ante 2 or 3 and see whether you find a useful basic Joker.

The real danger sign is:

```text
After Ante 3, you still have no stable scoring source.
```

At that point, blind requirements start rising quickly, and relying only on base poker hands becomes risky.

---

## 2. Shop Priority: Buy Survival Before Fancy Combos

### 2.1 Early Joker priority

In the early game, the most important Jokers are the ones that immediately increase your score. A rough priority order is:

```text
Stable +Mult Joker
> Stable +Chips Joker
> Economy Joker
> Slow scaling Joker
> Utility / gimmick Joker
```

If you have no scoring Joker at all, it is easy to die around Ante 2–3.  
Do not skip a simple but useful Joker just because it is not part of a perfect late-game combo.

### 2.2 Mid-to-late Joker structure

A stable White Stake winning setup often contains something like:

```text
1 Chips source
1–2 +Mult sources
1 XMult source
1 economy / utility / build-support Joker
```

You will not get this exact structure every run, but you should consciously look for your missing pieces.

### 2.3 Why XMult matters

Late-game blind requirements rise quickly.  
Plain `+Mult` becomes less impressive later, while `XMult` multiplies the Mult you already have. A good XMult source is often the difference between reaching Ante 8 and actually clearing it.

## 3. Economy: Do Not Spend All Your Money Every Round

### 3.1 Interest is important

At the end of each round, Balatro gives interest based on your money. By default, you gain $1 for every $5 you hold, up to $5 interest. This means you max out default interest at $25.

Practical guideline:

```text
Early game: buy survival Jokers when needed. Do not die just to preserve interest.
Mid game: try to stay around $15–$25 when possible.
Late game: if your build is stable, spend money on rerolls or key missing pieces.
```

### 3.2 Do not reroll mindlessly

A common beginner mistake is:

```text
The shop does not show what I want
→ reroll several times
→ lose all money
→ fail to become stronger
→ die on the next blind
```

A safer rule:

```text
money < 8: usually do not reroll, unless you are likely to die without doing so
money 8–15: allow only limited rerolls
money > 25: you can search more actively for key Jokers, Tarot cards, or Planet cards
```

### 3.3 Do not buy every Voucher you see

Vouchers can be powerful, but early money is tight.  
Be careful if buying a Voucher means you can no longer afford a scoring Joker or lose too much interest.

Higher-priority Vouchers include:

- Vouchers that increase hand size or discard count.
- Vouchers that improve your economy.
- Vouchers that strongly support your current build.

---

## 4. Hand Direction: Do Not Chase Too Many Plans at Once

### 4.1 Choose one or two main hands

Do not try to play every poker hand type. A stable White Stake run usually chooses one main scoring direction early, such as:

```text
Flush build
Pair / Two Pair build
High Card build
Full House build
```

Then you support it by:

```text
Buying the matching Planet cards
Using Tarot cards to fix your deck
Opening Standard Packs for useful cards
Choosing synergistic Jokers
```

### 4.2 Beginner-friendly hand routes

#### Flush route

Pros:

- Easy to assemble early.
- Does not require complex deck fixing at first.
- Good for learning the basic rhythm of the game.

Cons:

- Can become unstable later without XMult or deck manipulation.
- Some Boss Blinds can punish it.

#### Pair / Two Pair route

Pros:

- Easy to play consistently.
- Many scaling Jokers can grow from these hands.
- More reliable to trigger every round than some five-card hands.

Cons:

- Low base score.
- Needs Joker support for Chips and Mult.

#### High Card route

Pros:

- Extremely consistent; you can almost always play it.
- Many strong late-game builds can revolve around High Card.
- Less dependent on drawing a specific five-card combination.

Cons:

- Feels weak for beginners in the early game.
- Must rely heavily on Jokers rather than base hand value.

## 5. Planet, Tarot, and Pack Choices

### 5.1 Planet cards: upgrade hands you actually play

Do not buy Planet cards randomly. Prioritize:

```text
Planet for your current main hand
> Planet for a hand you are likely to transition into
> other Planet cards
```

If you are mainly playing Pair, do not spend money upgrading Straight or Flush unless you are truly planning to switch.

### 5.2 Tarot cards: prioritize deck fixing

Tarot cards are not only temporary value. Their deeper value is in modifying your deck.

Common goals:

- Increase the count of your main suit.
- Remove useless cards.
- Copy strong cards.
- Add enhancements to key cards.
- Modify cards so they work better with your Jokers.

### 5.3 Standard Pack and Buffoon Pack

Early Buffoon Packs are important because they help you find Jokers.  
Standard Packs can give you good cards with Seals, Editions, or Enhancements, but avoid opening too many packs when your economy is weak.

---

## 6. Skipping Blinds: Beginners Should Usually Avoid It

Skipping a blind gives you a Tag, but the cost is significant:

```text
You play one fewer round
You earn less money
You visit one fewer shop
You lose one chance to scale your build
```

Beginner guideline for White Stake:

```text
Do not skip most of the time.
```

Consider skipping only when:

- You see an Investment Tag early and the economy value is very high.
- You see a powerful Joker-related Tag, such as Negative, Polychrome, Holographic, or Foil.
- You see a Buffoon Tag and urgently need a Joker.
- Your build is already strong and you do not need the extra money/shop visit.

If you are unsure whether a Tag is worth skipping for, default to playing the blind normally.

## 7. Playing Hands: Do Not Only Chase the Highest Base Poker Hand

### 7.1 First ask what your build actually scores from

Different builds want different plays:

```text
If you rely on base hand value: play strong five-card hands.
If you rely on Joker triggers: play the hand that activates your Jokers.
If you rely on a specific enhanced card: make sure that card scores.
If you rely on scaling Jokers: trigger the scaling condition whenever safe.
```

For example, if you have a Joker that grows whenever you play a Pair, you may want to play Pair even when a Flush would score slightly more right now.

### 7.2 Do not waste hands and discards

General rules:

- If your current hand already clears the blind, consider conserving resources.
- If you need a key card, discard before playing.
- If you have a scaling Joker, trigger it when doing so is safe.

### 7.3 Remember Joker order

Joker order can affect scoring. A common rule is:

```text
Put +Mult Jokers toward the left
Put XMult Jokers toward the right
```

You usually want to add Mult first, then multiply it with XMult.

## 8. Stage-by-Stage White Stake Strategy

### Antes 1–2: Survival phase

Goal:

```text
Get your first stable scoring Joker
Avoid wasting money
Clear blinds with stable hands such as Flush, Full House, or Pair
```

Priorities:

- Buy cheap Jokers that immediately improve scoring.
- Open a small number of Buffoon Packs if you need Jokers.
- Do not sacrifice survival for a dream late-game combo.

### Antes 3–5: Build formation phase

Goal:

```text
Choose your main hand direction
Start upgrading the matching Planet
Build an economy
Add Chips and Mult sources
```

Priorities:

- Commit to a main route.
- Buy scaling Jokers when they fit your route.
- Control reroll frequency.
- Use Tarot and Standard Packs to improve your deck.

### Antes 6–8: Power spike phase

Goal:

```text
Find XMult
Patch weak parts of your build
Prepare for Boss Blinds
```

Priorities:

- Save enough money to search for key pieces.
- Add XMult if you do not already have it.
- Check whether the Boss Blind counters your main route.
- Sell weak Jokers if you need room for stronger ones.

## 9. Common Reasons for Dying

### 9.1 You have Chips but not enough Mult

Your score may feel stable for a while, but it will not scale enough later.

### 9.2 You have +Mult but no XMult

This often works through the mid game, then fails around Ante 7–8.

### 9.3 Your economy collapses

If you spend all your money every round, you eventually cannot:

```text
Buy key Jokers
Open important Packs
Reroll for missing pieces
Earn interest
```

### 9.4 You chase too many hand types

Trying to play Flush, Straight, Full House, and Four of a Kind at the same time usually means none of them becomes strong enough.

### 9.5 You overvalue rare Jokers

White Stake does not require a god-tier combo every run.  
Many ordinary Joker combinations can clear the game if the scoring structure is complete.

## 10. Executable Rule Summary for an AI Agent

These rules can be used as a simple baseline for a Balatro-playing AI agent.

### 10.1 Shop rules

```text
If Joker count < 2:
    Prioritize cheap +Mult or +Chips Jokers.

If there is no +Mult source:
    Prioritize stable +Mult Jokers.

If there is no Chips source:
    Prioritize Chips Jokers or Planet upgrades for the main hand.

If Ante >= 5 and there is no XMult source:
    Increase the priority of XMult Jokers.

If money < 8:
    Do not reroll unless the next blind is likely fatal.

If money >= 25:
    Allow rerolls to search for key components.
```

### 10.2 Hand-route rules

```text
If there is existing Flush synergy:
    Main route = Flush.
    Prioritize matching Planet cards and suit-changing Tarot cards.

If there is Pair / Two Pair synergy:
    Main route = Pair / Two Pair.
    Prioritize keeping duplicate ranks.

If Jokers mainly scale on frequent triggers:
    Prefer stable hands such as High Card or Pair.
```

### 10.3 Play-phase rules

```text
Enumerate all playable card combinations.
Estimate immediate score.

If the blind can be cleared:
    Prefer the option that consumes fewer resources or triggers useful scaling.

If the blind cannot be cleared yet:
    Choose the highest expected score line.
```

### 10.4 Blind-skip rules

```text
Default: do not skip.

Only skip when the Tag is high value and survival pressure is low.
Investment, Negative, Polychrome, and Buffoon Tags can increase skip priority.
```

## 11. White Stake Checklist

At the end of each Ante, ask:

```text
Do I have stable +Mult?
Do I have a Chips source?
Do I have late-game XMult?
What is my main hand route?
Is my money always below $5?
Do at least three of my Jokers directly contribute to scoring?
Am I destroying my economy by opening packs or rerolling too much?
Can the next Boss Blind counter my main route?
```

If you can answer most of these questions clearly, your White Stake clear rate should improve a lot.

---

## 12. Shortest Version

```text
Buy scoring Jokers early.
Do not waste your economy.
Focus on one main hand type.
Build around Chips + Mult + XMult.
Do not skip blinds or reroll carelessly.
Find XMult before the late game.
```
